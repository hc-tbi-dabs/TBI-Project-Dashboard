Overall Project Health

The interpretation of the color section is based on the status of the core project management metrics as follows:


Green On scope, on schedule, on budget and managed risks.
Yellow Slightly behind schedule or slightly over budget, with some impact on milestones, but corrective actions proposed and with most risks under control.
Red In serious difficulty and needs immediate attention. Significant intervention required.
■ ■
2 Reds force the overall project health to "RED"
■
1 Red force the overall project health to "YELLOW"
■ ■
2 Yellows force the overall project health to "YELLOW"
■ ■ ■
3 Yellows force the overall project health to "RED"
■ ■
1 Yellow plus 1 Red force the overall project health to "RED"
■ ■ ■
2 Yellow plus 1 Red force the overall project health to "RED"
■ ■ ■ ■ ■
Most other scenarios will result in the overall project health of "GREEN"

Individual Component Metric Status

Cost
  • Variance of less than 10%.
  • project is progressing on track and the intended business outcomes are expected to be achieved.
  • Project completion is expected within budget.
  • Variance of between 10% and 20%.
  • Some course correction may be required.
  • One of more of the intended business outcomes may not be possible within budget.
  • Additional funding or re-profiling may be required.
  • Variance of over 20%.
  • Significant course correction may be required.
  • One of more of the intended business outcomes may not be possible within the budget.
  • Funding decisions are required.
Schedule
  • Variance of less than 10%.
  • The project is progressing on track and the intended business outcomes are expecte to be achieved.
  • Project completion is expected within the planned schedule.
  • Variance of between 10% and 20%.
  • Some course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended business outcomes may not be achieved.
  • Project completion may not be achieved.
  • Project completion may not be possible within the planned schedule.
  • Variance of over 20%.
  • Significant course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended business outcomes may not be achieved.
  • Project completion may not be possible within the planned schedule.
  • Scheduling decisions are required.

Scope
  • The project is on track.
  • The intended business outcomes are expected to be achieved.
  • Identified changes are not expected to negatively impact the project's scope, cost or schedule.
  • Some course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended business outcomes may not be achieved.
  • Identified changes may negatively impact the project's scope, cost or schedule and some course correction may be required.
  • Significant course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended business outcomes may not be achieved.
  • Identified changes may negatively impact the project's scope, cost or schedule and significant course correction may be required.
Risk
  • The project is on track.
  • The identified risks are not expected to impact theother project metrics or overall business outcomes.
  • Some course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended risks may impact the other project metrics or overall business outcomes and some course correction may be requried.
  • Significant course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended risks may impact the other project metrics or overall business outcomes and significant course correction may be required.
Issues
  • The project is on track.
  • The intended business outcomes are expected to be achieved.
  • Identified changes are not expected to negatively impact the project's scope, cost or schedule.
  • Some course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended business outcomes may not be achieved.
  • Identified changes may negatively impact the project's scope, cost or schedule and some course correction may be required.
  • Significant course correction may be required.
  • One or more of the intended business outcomes may not be achieved.
  • Identified changes may negatively impact the project's scope, cost or schedule and significant course correction may be required.

Individual Risk Classification

Individual Risks - Impact
Minor impact on the project, e.g. < 5% deviation in scope, scheduled end-date or project budget.
Measurable impact on the project, e.g. 5-20% deviation in scope, scheduled end-date or project budget.
Major impact on the project, e.g. > 25% deviation in scope, scheduled end-date or project budget.
Individual Risks - Probability
Unlikely to occur, based on current information, as the circumstances likely to trigger the risk are also unlikely to occur.
Likely to occur as it is clear that the risk will probably materialize.
Highly likely to occur as the circumstances which will cause this risk to materialize are also very likely to be created.

Individual Issues - Impact
Indicates that issues are under control and well managed. The following are in place, up-to-date and actively managed:
  • Resolution Plan and Target date for resolution;
  • Ownership identified and accepted, and;
  • All identified issues are manageable within the scope, cost and schedule baselines.
Indicates caution as issues are not being adequately addressed. One or more of the following might be missing:
  • Resolution plan;
  • Target date for resolution;
  • Ownership identified and accepted, and;
  • One or more of the identified issues are potentially unmanageable within the scope, cost or schedule baselines.
Indicates alert, as issues are not being managed, or the resolution activities undertaken to date have not been effective in resolving the issues, and objectives are being impacted - serious attention is required to resolve the issues. One or more of the following is not in place, up-to-date or actively managed:
  • Resolution plan;
  • Target date for resoltuion;
  • Ownership identified and accepted, and;
  • One or more of the identified issues will seriously affect the scope, cost or schedule baselines.
